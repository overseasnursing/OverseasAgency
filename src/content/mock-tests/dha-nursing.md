## Introduction to the DHA Nursing Exam

The **DHA (Dubai Health Authority) nursing exam** is the mandatory licensing examination for all internationally trained nurses who want to work legally in Dubai, UAE. If you are an Indian nurse planning your overseas migration, passing the DHA exam is your first official milestone on the path to a Dubai nursing career.

Dubai consistently ranks as one of the top destinations for Indian nurses, offering **tax-free salaries**, free accommodation in most government hospitals, annual flight allowances, and comprehensive medical insurance. A DHA licence is your gateway to all of this.

This guide covers everything you need — from eligibility and the DataFlow process to the exam syllabus, a 30-day study plan, fees, common mistakes, and what happens after you pass.

## What is the DHA Nursing Exam?

The Dubai Health Authority (DHA) is the regulatory body responsible for licensing all healthcare professionals in the Emirate of Dubai. The DHA nursing exam is a **computer-based, multiple-choice examination** that verifies internationally trained nurses meet Dubai's clinical competency standards.

**Key facts at a glance:**
- Administered by **Prometric** at authorised centres across India
- 150 MCQs over 165 minutes
- Tests knowledge across all major nursing specialties
- A valid DHA licence is required before you can legally work as a nurse in any Dubai healthcare facility
- DHA licences are valid for **2 years** and must be renewed

> **Important:** The DHA licence is specific to Dubai. To work in Abu Dhabi, you need the DOH licence. For other Emirates, you need the MOH licence.

## DHA Eligibility Criteria

Before applying, confirm that you meet all eligibility requirements. Submitting an incomplete application wastes time and money.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Nursing Degree | BSc Nursing (4-year) from a recognised institution |
| Clinical Experience | Minimum 2 years post-qualification clinical experience |
| Home Country Licence | Valid nursing licence from your State Nursing Council |
| DataFlow Verification | Mandatory Primary Source Verification |
| English Proficiency | No separate English test required for DHA |
| Good Standing | No disciplinary actions or licence suspensions |

**Notes for Indian nurses:**
- Your nursing registration certificate from the State Nursing Council must be valid at the time of application
- If you have gaps in employment, be prepared to explain them with documentation
- GNM diploma holders should check current DHA eligibility requirements directly on the Sheryan portal, as policies are updated periodically

## DHA Registration Process

The DHA application process involves multiple steps across different systems. Follow each step carefully to avoid delays.

**Step 1 — Create Your Sheryan Account**
Visit the official DHA Sheryan portal and create a healthcare professional account. Enter your personal details, education history, and work experience accurately.

**Step 2 — Submit Your Application**
Upload all required documents: passport copy, nursing degree and transcripts, home-country nursing licence, employment certificates (2+ years), and a recent passport photograph.

**Step 3 — Pay the Application Fee**
Pay the DHA application processing fee. Always verify the current fee on the Sheryan portal, as amounts are updated periodically.

**Step 4 — Receive Your DataFlow Reference Number**
After initial review, DHA sends you a DataFlow reference number. This triggers the credential verification process.

**Step 5 — Complete DataFlow Verification**
Submit your documents through the DataFlow portal and pay the verification fee. This process takes 8–12 weeks on average.

**Step 6 — Receive Your ATT Letter**
Once DataFlow clears, DHA sends your Authorisation to Test (ATT) letter from Prometric. Use this to schedule your exam slot at a Prometric centre near you.

**Step 7 — Sit the Exam and Receive Results**
Attend your scheduled exam with valid photo ID. Results are typically available immediately after the exam or within 24–48 hours on the Prometric portal.

## DataFlow Verification Process

DataFlow is a non-negotiable step in the DHA process. It independently verifies the authenticity of your credentials by contacting your educational institution, nursing council, and employers directly.

| DataFlow Step | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| Registration | Create account at dataflowgroup.com |
| Documents Required | Degree, transcripts, nursing licence, passport, employment letters |
| Verification Fee | Approximately USD 140–180 (check current rates) |
| Processing Time | 8–12 weeks (can be longer if documents are incomplete) |
| Output | Primary Source Verification (PSV) report sent directly to DHA |

**Tips to avoid DataFlow delays:**
- Scan documents clearly — blurry photos cause rejection
- Ensure your nursing council sends the licence verification directly to DataFlow (not through you)
- Check your DataFlow portal regularly for requests for additional documents
- Use an email address you check daily — DataFlow sends time-sensitive requests

## DHA Nursing Syllabus

The DHA exam tests knowledge across all core nursing domains. Allocate your study time according to the approximate weightage of each subject:

| Subject | Approximate Weightage | Priority Level |
|---------|----------------------|----------------|
| Medical-Surgical Nursing | 25–30% | Critical |
| Fundamentals of Nursing | 15–20% | Critical |
| Pharmacology | 15% | Critical |
| Infection Control & Patient Safety | 10% | High |
| Paediatric Nursing | 8–10% | High |
| Maternity / Obstetric Nursing | 8–10% | High |
| Community Health Nursing | 5–8% | Medium |
| Psychiatric & Mental Health Nursing | 5% | Medium |
| Nursing Management & Leadership | 3–5% | Low |

**High-yield topics to focus on:**

**Medical-Surgical** — Cardiovascular disorders (MI, heart failure, hypertension), respiratory conditions (pneumonia, COPD, asthma), neurological nursing (stroke, seizures, raised ICP), renal nursing (CKD, dialysis), endocrine disorders (diabetes, thyroid), gastrointestinal nursing, oncology basics.

**Pharmacology** — Drug classifications, mechanisms of action, nursing implications, safe dosage calculations, high-alert medications (insulin, heparin, warfarin, digoxin), drug interactions.

**Infection Control** — Standard precautions, transmission-based precautions (airborne, droplet, contact), hand hygiene, PPE use, healthcare-associated infections (HAIs), sterilisation vs disinfection methods.

## DHA Passing Score

The DHA exam uses a **scaled scoring system**. A raw score of approximately **60% (around 90 out of 150 correct answers)** is generally considered passing. The exact passing standard is set by DHA.

**If you do not pass:**
- You will receive a domain-by-domain score breakdown
- Waiting period before reattempting: typically 45–90 days
- No official limit on the number of attempts, but each attempt requires the full exam fee
- Use the breakdown to identify and target your weakest domains

**After passing:**
- DHA issues your licence within 2–4 weeks
- Licence validity: 2 years from date of issue
- Your employer in Dubai handles work permit and visa processing using your DHA licence number

## 30-Day Study Plan

A focused 4-week plan for candidates starting from scratch:

| Week | Focus Area | Daily Commitment |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Week 1 | Fundamentals of Nursing + Infection Control | 2 hours theory + 30 timed MCQs |
| Week 2 | Medical-Surgical Part 1 (Cardiovascular, Respiratory, Neurology) | 2.5 hours theory + 40 MCQs |
| Week 3 | Medical-Surgical Part 2 (Renal, Endocrine, GI) + Pharmacology | 2.5 hours theory + 50 MCQs |
| Week 4 | Paediatrics, Maternity, Community Health + Full Mock Tests | 2 hours review + 2 full mock tests per day |

**Week 4 is your most important week.** Do not study new topics. Use this week entirely for full-length, timed mock tests and reviewing incorrect answers. Identify patterns in your mistakes — are they knowledge gaps or time-management errors?

## Common Mistakes Candidates Make

Learning from others' failures is the most efficient form of preparation:

**1. Only studying theory without MCQ practice**
Knowing the theory is not enough for DHA. You must be able to apply knowledge under time pressure. Start MCQ practice from Day 1, not just in the final week.

**2. Underestimating pharmacology**
Many candidates skip or skim pharmacology because it feels vast. With 15% weightage, it is one of the highest-yield subjects. Focus on high-alert medications and nursing implications.

**3. Not practising timed exams**
66 seconds per question sounds like plenty until you are sitting in the test centre. Candidates who have never practised under time pressure consistently run out of time.

**4. Using outdated study materials**
Clinical guidelines and evidence-based protocols change. An edition from 2018 may contain guidelines that are now considered incorrect. Use updated materials.

**5. Neglecting infection control**
With 10% weightage, infection control is straightforward, predictable, and high-yield. Standard precautions, isolation types, and hand hygiene are frequently tested.

**6. Poor exam-day preparation**
Over-studying the night before leads to fatigue and impaired recall. Sleep 7–8 hours the night before the exam. Arrive at the test centre 30 minutes early. Eat a proper meal.
